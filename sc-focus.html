SOUNDCLOUD FOCUS

FEATURE DESIGN

Overview

A "focus timer" feature for SoundCloud's mobile application, designed and prototyped as part of a senior-level interaction design course.

Duration: 3 weeks

Tools: Figma, ProtoPie

VIEW PROTOTYPE

Background Research

While I knew of long-time SoundCloud users around me, I myself began this project with minimal experience in using the service before.

In order to understand and analyze the ways SoundCloud was used, I conducted interviews over Zoom and Discord with university students ages 20-22 to answer who they were as consumers, as well as why and when they use SoundCloud.

A summary of key insights from interviews.

User Persona

Many of the interviewees mentioned that while they open SoundCloud when they intend to be productive, they usually leave the app when they get distracted from whatever task they chose to accomplish, even if they did not mean to. Furthermore, many of them mentioned that mainly open SoundCloud with the intentions of listening to playlists or artists that they already had added to their library.

A user persona based upon issues and goals discovered during interviews.

Idea proposal

Seeing that outside distractions seemed to be the leading obstacle in user retention, I decided a fun way to prevent this issue was by proposing a focus timer feature. This would work so that users are able to combine one of their pre-existing playlists with a count-down timer so that they are encouraged to retain their focus for a set amount of time, therefore minimizing their likelihood of leaving the app due to distractions.

Titled "focus mode", made to address the problems felt by the user persona.

Prototype

After a feature had been proposed, I created three rounds of wireframes in the span of one week, from low fidelity to high. Throughout the process, I was able to get casual feedback from my peers as to what parts of the interaction were unclear to them, which helped me greatly in identifying which parts of the interactions I should focus on iterating upon given the time constraint.

First round of wireframes, done on paper with pencil.

Mid-fidelity wireframes showing the primary user flow.

High-fidelity wireframes used as a base for the final prototype.

Design System

One of the biggest challenges during this project was that as an unaffiliated student, I did not have access to SoundCloud's style guides. To ensure that this would not result in gaps between my feature's and SoundCloud's design systems, I opted to do an intensive analysis of the of the app in order to fine a reference for every new screen I was to include in my feature.

The song info + Exit overlay. Elements referenced from user library screen as well as the app's original song info screen.

The feature home page, with multiple elements referenced from the user library screen.

The activity + duration selection screen, with elements referenced from playlist information screens.

Features

Listeners will be able to plan a session by picking a playlist and setting their timer. They can also choose to log additional information such as their activities, or be able to revisit a previous session from the menu screen to maximize their productivity.

Plan your session

Before using the timer, listeners are asked to set a time and a playlist to use during the session. Returning users are able to pick from a previously planned session, or view their previous activity to guide them in planning a new one.

Outcome

This project was important to me in understanding the process behind designing new products for a pre-existing service. From identifying user bases, to integrating my designs with a pre-existing design system, I feel that SoundCloud Focus is an accurate portrayal of my ability to become somewhat of a "chameleon" and fully immerse myself with any business within a short period of time.
